My Coffee Brewing Setup

January 10, 2026 · 5 min read

My morning ritual starts before I open my laptop. The hiss of steam, the aroma of freshly ground beans, the first sip of a perfectly pulled shot — coffee isn't just fuel, it's a craft I've been refining for years.

The Grinder is Everything

If you take one thing from this post: spend more on your grinder than your machine. A consistent grind size matters more than any feature on an espresso machine. I use a flat-burr grinder that gives me uniform particle distribution, which means even extraction and balanced flavor.

Pre-ground coffee loses its aromatics within minutes. Whole beans, stored in a vacuum canister, stay vibrant for weeks. The difference is night and day.

My Current Gear

  • Grinder: Flat-burr electric grinder (stepless adjustment)
  • Machine: Dual-boiler with PID temperature control
  • Scale: 0.1g precision with built-in timer
  • Kettle: Gooseneck for pour-overs on weekends
  • Beans: Rotating single-origins from Ethiopia, Colombia, and Kenya

The Recipe

My daily espresso: 18g in, 36g out, in about 28 seconds. But here's the secret — I adjust by taste, not numbers. If it's sour, I grind finer. If it's bitter, I grind coarser. The scale and timer are training wheels; eventually your palate becomes the guide.

"Coffee is a conversation between the bean and the water. Your job is to listen."

Beyond Espresso

On slower mornings I reach for a V60 pour-over. The ritual of the bloom, the spiral pour, the patience — it's meditative. I keep a journal of each bag: origin, roast date, grind setting, tasting notes. Looking back at six months of entries, I can trace my evolving preferences like a graph.
